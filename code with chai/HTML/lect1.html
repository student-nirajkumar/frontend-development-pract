Bhagat singh-The worriers

Bhagat Singh, one of India's most iconic freedom fighters, is remembered as a fearless and passionate revolutionary who played a pivotal role in the Indian independence movement. Born on September 28, 1907, in a small village in Punjab, Bhagat Singh was deeply influenced by the atrocities committed by the British rulers. His early exposure to the freedom struggle and his admiration for martyrs like Kartar Singh Sarabha fueled his desire to fight for India's independence. Bhagat Singh is best known for his bold and courageous acts against British colonial rule, including his involvement in the bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly in 1929 and his participation in the killing of John Saunders, a British police officer. He became a symbol of youthful defiance and revolutionary zeal, believing in armed resistance against the oppressive British rule. Despite his young age, his ideologies and writings inspired countless Indians to rise up against colonialism.
